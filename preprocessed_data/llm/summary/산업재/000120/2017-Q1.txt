  - Drivers claim that the agency deducts around 10% from their earnings, leaving them with only 700 KRW per delivery.

  - The delivery system involves a hierarchical structure where delivery companies contract agencies, and agencies employ delivery drivers.
  - Drivers are affiliated with agencies rather than the delivery companies directly.

  - The agency allegedly charges fees more than double the usual rate of 8-10%.
  - It is accused of blocking drivers' access to digital records and concealing delivery details to generate additional profits of approximately 10 million KRW monthly.

  - Delivery drivers are described as the weakest link in the industry, lacking the power to challenge imposed fees or payment structures.
  - A driver who raised concerns faced contract termination, leading to a delivery strike by other drivers.

- **Agency's Defense and Legal Context:**
  - The agency claims that fees are determined based on delivery distance and workload, with each agency setting its own standards.
  - Current laws do not explicitly regulate agency fees, allowing for subjective application by agency owners without legal repercussions.

- **Call for Administrative Intervention:**
  - The growing conflict between the agency and drivers highlights the need for administrative oversight and mediation to address the issue.
