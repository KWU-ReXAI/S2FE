**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Developments:**

1. **Korean Air's Capital Increase:**
   - Korean Air is facing a management crisis and has decided to undertake a capital increase, the largest since its founding.
   - The company plans to raise 1 trillion KRW through a rights offering by the end of July.
   - New shares to be issued: 79,365,079 shares at an expected price of 12,600 KRW per share.
   - The rights offering will prioritize existing shareholders before a public offering.
   - Korean Air also plans to sell assets, such as the Songhyeon-dong site, to improve liquidity.
   - Hanjin KAL, the major shareholder, is discussing participation in the capital increase.

2. **Doosan Group's Self-Rescue Plan:**
   - Doosan Group is also in financial difficulty and is expected to announce a self-rescue plan soon.
   - Doosan Corporation is holding a board meeting to discuss a 3 trillion KRW self-rescue plan.
   - The plan involves asset sales and cost reductions to secure liquidity.
   - There is speculation about the sale of key subsidiaries like Doosan Infracore and Doosan Bobcat.
   - The potential sale of Doosan Tower, a landmark in Dongdaemun, is also being considered.
   - The accounting firm Samil PwC is conducting due diligence and will report to creditors, including the Korea Development Bank, as early as next week.
