### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Developments

#### 대한항공 (Korean Air)
- **Capital Increase**: Korean Air is planning a significant capital increase, the largest in its history, amounting to 1 trillion KRW. This involves issuing 79,365,079 new shares at an expected price of 12,600 KRW per share.
- **Methodology**: The capital increase will be conducted by offering shares to existing shareholders first, followed by a public offering. The new shares are expected to be listed by the end of July.
- **Asset Sales**: In addition to the capital increase, Korean Air plans to sell assets, such as the Songhyeon-dong site, to improve liquidity.
- **Major Shareholder Involvement**: Hanjin KAL, the major shareholder of Korean Air, held a board meeting to discuss participation in the capital increase, with details to be disclosed later.

#### 두산그룹 (Doosan Group)
- **Financial Restructuring Plan**: Doosan Group is expected to announce a financial restructuring plan worth 3 trillion KRW. This plan aims to stabilize the group's financial situation.
- **Asset Sales**: The group has previously submitted a plan to creditors to secure liquidity by selling assets and reducing costs. However, specific details were not included, leading to various speculations.
- **Potential Asset Sales**: There is significant attention on the potential sale of key subsidiaries, Doosan Infracore and Doosan Bobcat, as well as the landmark Doosan Tower in Dongdaemun.
- **Creditor Involvement**: The accounting firm Samil PwC is conducting due diligence on Doosan Group's assets and is expected to report findings to creditors, including the Korea Development Bank and the Export-Import Bank of Korea, as early as next week.
