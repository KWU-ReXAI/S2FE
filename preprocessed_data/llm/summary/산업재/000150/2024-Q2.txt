**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

- **Criticism of Corporate Governance Practices:**
  - Lee Bok-hyun, the head of the Financial Supervisory Service, criticized corporate management practices that prioritize the interests of controlling shareholders over other stakeholders.
  - He described these practices as a recurring issue that undermines corporate growth and competitiveness, despite government efforts to improve these areas.

- **Specific Case: Doosan Group:**
  - The criticism is interpreted as being directed at Doosan Group, which is undergoing a corporate restructuring.
  - Doosan Group plans to merge its subsidiary, Doosan Bobcat, with Doosan Robotics, a move seen as prioritizing the interests of controlling shareholders.
  - This restructuring involves exchanging the profitable Doosan Bobcat with the loss-making Doosan Robotics, leading to shareholder dissent and claims of interest infringement.

- **Regulatory Response:**
  - The Financial Supervisory Service has requested Doosan to amend its securities report, indicating regulatory scrutiny.
  - Despite Doosan submitting a revised report, the head of the Financial Supervisory Service continues to voice concerns, suggesting ongoing regulatory challenges for Doosan.

- **Implications for Stakeholders:**
  - Doosan must navigate the regulatory landscape and convince stakeholders, including the National Pension Service, of the merits of its restructuring plan.
  - The situation highlights the broader issue of corporate governance reform and the need for balancing shareholder interests.
