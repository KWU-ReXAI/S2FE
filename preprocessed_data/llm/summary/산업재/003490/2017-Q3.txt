- **Introduction of Biofuel in Aviation**: A domestic airline has operated a passenger flight using biofuel derived from plants like corn for the first time. This marks a significant step in the aviation industry's efforts to adopt more sustainable fuel sources.

- **Flight Details**: The flight in question was Korean Air flight KEO38, which departed from Chicago Airport and arrived at Incheon Airport after a 14-hour journey. The aircraft used a fuel mixture consisting of 95% traditional aviation fuel (ZU) and 5% biofuel.

- **Industry Projections**: The aviation industry anticipates that biofuel will become more widely adopted starting in 2020. By 2050, it is expected that biofuel will account for 25% of the total aviation fuel market, reflecting a significant shift towards more environmentally friendly fuel options.

- **Environmental Impact**: The use of biofuel is part of a broader strategy to reduce the carbon footprint of air travel and promote sustainability within the industry.
