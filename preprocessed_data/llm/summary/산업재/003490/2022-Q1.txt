**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

- **Airbus Lobbying Scandal (2016):** 
  - Airbus, a European aircraft manufacturer, was involved in a lobbying scandal where it allegedly paid rebates to various airlines, including Korean Air, to sell its aircraft.

- **Allegations Against Korean Air:**
  - Korean Air was accused of receiving approximately 18 billion won (around 15 million USD) in rebates from Airbus.
  - The rebates were allegedly paid to high-ranking executives at Korean Air as part of three sales contracts between 1996 and 2000 for a total of 10 aircraft.

- **Investigation and Legal Proceedings:**
  - The French Financial Prosecutor's Office and Airbus entered into a public judicial agreement in January 2020, where Airbus cooperated with the investigation to reduce its penalties.
  - The European Joint Investigation Team found that Airbus made payments to Korean Air through intermediaries or donations to a U.S. university associated with Korean Air.

- **Korean Legal Actions:**
  - In 2020, a South Korean lawmaker raised the issue in the National Assembly, leading to legal actions against Korean Air executives, including Cho Won-tae and Cho Hyun-ah, for embezzlement and breach of trust.
  - The Seoul Central District Prosecutors' Office requested judicial cooperation from the European investigation team and received related materials by the end of the previous year.

- **Current Investigation Focus:**
  - The investigation is examining the involvement of Korean Air's owner family in the rebate process and the flow of the funds received.

- **Korean Air's Response:**
  - Korean Air's current management denies any involvement in the Airbus rebate allegations.
  - They argue that the agreement document with Airbus was merely a settlement to avoid penalties and does not confirm any factual evidence of wrongdoing.
