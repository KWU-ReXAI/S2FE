**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Data:**

- **Ownership of Stocks by Minors in Large Conglomerates:**
  - A total of 43 minors from the families of major conglomerate owners in South Korea hold significant shares in listed companies.
  - The average stock ownership per minor is approximately 2.37 billion KRW.

- **Data Source and Date:**
  - The data was provided by Park Kwang-on, a member of the Democratic Party of Korea, based on information from the Korea Fair Trade Commission.
  - The data reflects the status as of April 1st of the current year.

- **Distribution Among Conglomerates:**
  - Doosan Group has the highest number of minors with stock ownership, totaling 7.
  - GS Group follows with 5 minors, and LS Group has 3 minors holding stocks worth several billion KRW each.

This summary highlights the concentration of wealth and stock ownership among minors in South Korea's major business families, indicating potential implications for economic inequality and corporate governance.
