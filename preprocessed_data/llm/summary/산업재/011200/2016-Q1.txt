**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

1. **Debt Restructuring Challenges:**
   - Hyundai Merchant Marine (HMM) is facing significant challenges in its efforts to restructure its debt.
   - The company is seeking to extend the maturity of its corporate bonds, a critical step for its ongoing self-rescue plan.
   - A previous attempt to extend the maturity of a 120 billion won bond was overwhelmingly rejected by bondholders.

2. **Upcoming Bondholder Meetings:**
   - HMM has scheduled a bondholder meeting starting on the 31st to discuss the extension of bond maturities.
   - The company is appealing to individual bondholders to extend the maturity of bonds worth 810 billion won due next year.

3. **Potential Consequences of Failure:**
   - Failure to secure an agreement on debt restructuring could lead to court receivership for HMM.

4. **Proposed Debt Restructuring Terms:**
   - HMM has proposed a two-year grace period followed by a three-year installment repayment plan.
   - There is criticism that the proposal lacks sufficient equity conversion, with more than 50% equity conversion being suggested.

5. **Lease Fee Negotiations:**
   - HMM is also negotiating to reduce its charter hire fees by 28.4%, which is crucial for its financial restructuring.
   - The outcome of these negotiations will influence the debt restructuring plan for the company's creditors.

6. **Stakeholder Involvement:**
   - HMM has engaged in preliminary discussions with institutional investors regarding the debt restructuring plan.
   - The success of the plan largely depends on the agreement of bondholders.

7. **Broader Implications:**
   - The results of the lease fee negotiations and bondholder meetings will significantly impact HMM's financial stability and future operations.
