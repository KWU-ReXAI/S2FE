- **Contract Termination and Reduction:**

  - Affected companies express dissatisfaction, labeling KAI's actions as unilateral and typical of large corporations exerting undue influence.

- **Specific Case and Allegations:**
  - A company supplying aircraft wing components to KAI received a contract termination notice in July.
  - The company claims the termination was to favor another specific company, criticizing it as corporate misconduct.
  - KAI counters that the supplier violated contract terms by receiving a guilty verdict for bribing a KAI employee with 300 million won, justifying the contract termination.

- **Broader Supplier Impact:**
  - Multiple suppliers report reductions in order volumes, which were traditionally automatically renewed barring significant issues.
  - Suppliers express frustration over the sudden and unilateral nature of these reductions.

- **KAI's Justification:**
  - KAI explains that it must allocate more orders to key partners and reduce orders for companies capable of independent survival.
  - The company frames these decisions as necessary for enhancing competitiveness.

- **Diverging Perspectives:**
  - There is a tension between viewing KAI's actions as a strategic necessity for competitiveness and as a manipulative tactic to control suppliers.
  - The affected companies are experiencing significant distress due to these changes.
