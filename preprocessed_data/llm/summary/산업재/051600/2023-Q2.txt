### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:

- **Emergency Power Supply Inspection:**
  - Korea Electric Power Service Corporation (KEPCO KPS) conducted an emergency inspection of the power supply system in response to heatwaves and typhoons.
  - The focus was on ensuring a stable power supply through the inspection of thermal and nuclear power generation and transmission facilities.

- **Maintenance and Safety Measures:**
  - KEPCO KPS implemented measures to maintain and manage the safety of on-site personnel.
  - The company reviewed and updated emergency recovery systems to enhance readiness.

- **Resource Allocation:**
  - KEPCO KPS deployed over 730 emergency maintenance personnel across 64 sites.
  - These personnel are on a 24-hour rotational standby to ensure rapid response to any power supply issues.

### Logical Structure:

1. **Introduction:**
   - Context of extreme weather conditions (heatwaves and typhoons) prompting action.

2. **Inspection and Maintenance:**
   - Details of the inspection process for power generation and transmission facilities.
   - Emphasis on the stability of power supply and safety management.

3. **Emergency Preparedness:**
   - Deployment of emergency personnel and establishment of a 24-hour standby system.
   - Focus on rapid response capabilities to maintain power supply stability.
