### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments

1. **Hyundai Glovis' Major Contracts:**
   - Hyundai Glovis has secured a significant maritime transport contract worth approximately 1.045 trillion KRW with a global automobile manufacturer, effective from January 2024 to the end of 2024.
   - In September, Hyundai Glovis also signed another substantial contract with a different global car brand, valued at around 2.2 trillion KRW, spanning three years starting in 2023.

2. **Fleet Expansion and Investment:**
   - The company has been investing in expanding its fleet, currently owning over 90 car carriers.
   - Hyundai Glovis has introduced the Post-Panamax car carrier, capable of transporting up to 7,300 small cars, enhancing its competitive edge by allowing the transport of larger volumes at competitive rates.

3. **Adaptation to Electric Vehicle (EV) Market:**
   - In response to the growing electric vehicle market, Hyundai Glovis has developed a tailored maritime transport solution specifically for EVs.
   - This includes marking EVs on shipping documents to ensure they receive specialized handling, distinct from internal combustion engine vehicles.

4. **Shift in Revenue Composition:**
   - Hyundai Glovis has been actively reducing its reliance on affiliate company revenues in its maritime transport business.
   - The proportion of non-affiliate revenue has increased significantly, from 12% in 2010 to 61% in 2021, indicating a strategic shift towards diversifying its client base and reducing dependency on affiliated companies.

### Analysis and Context

- **Strategic Positioning:**
  - Hyundai Glovis is strategically positioning itself as a leader in the maritime transport sector by expanding its fleet and adopting innovative solutions tailored to the evolving automotive market, particularly the rise of electric vehicles.
  
- **Competitive Advantage:**
  - The introduction of the Post-Panamax car carrier and the EV-specific transport solutions provide Hyundai Glovis with a competitive advantage in terms of cost efficiency and market responsiveness.

- **Market Diversification:**
  - The shift towards increasing non-affiliate revenue highlights Hyundai Glovis' efforts to diversify its market presence and reduce risk associated with over-reliance on a limited client base.
