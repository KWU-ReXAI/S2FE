**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Developments:**

1. **Contract Announcements:**
   - Hyundai Glovis has secured a significant maritime transport contract worth approximately 1.045 trillion KRW with a global automaker, effective from January 2024 to the end of 2024.
   - Previously, in September, Hyundai Glovis signed another major contract with a different global automaker for maritime transport services valued at around 2.2 trillion KRW over three years starting in 2023.

2. **Fleet Expansion and Investment:**
   - Hyundai Glovis has been investing in expanding its fleet, currently owning over 90 vessels.
   - The company has introduced the Post-Panamax car carrier, capable of transporting up to 7,300 small cars, enhancing its competitive edge by allowing the transport of larger volumes at competitive rates.

3. **Adaptation to Market Trends:**
   - In response to the growing electric vehicle (EV) market, Hyundai Glovis has developed a tailored maritime transport solution specifically for EVs, marking a first in the global car carrier market.
   - This solution includes distinct management processes for EVs, differentiated from internal combustion engine vehicles during transport.

4. **Strategic Business Shifts:**
   - Hyundai Glovis is actively reducing its reliance on affiliate company revenues in its maritime transport business.
   - The proportion of non-affiliate revenue has increased significantly from 12% in 2010 to 61% in 2021, indicating a strategic shift towards diversifying its client base and revenue streams.
