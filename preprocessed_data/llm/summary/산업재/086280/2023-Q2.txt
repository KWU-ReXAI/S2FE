**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Market Analysis:**

1. **Market Overview:**
   - The market started at the high points of the previous Thursday and Friday.
   - Semiconductor stocks supported the index, while secondary batteries and automotive stocks declined.
   - Service sectors, including Naver and Samsung SDS, showed strength, along with insurance and food sectors.

2. **Foreign and Institutional Investors:**
   - Foreign investors consistently bought futures, reducing open interest.
   - Institutional investors, particularly pension funds, were net sellers, impacting the market negatively.
   - The focus was on whether foreign investors would reduce their futures selling, which was not fully realized.

3. **Sector Performance:**
   - Secondary batteries and related sectors need to decline further for a market rebound.
   - Undervalued stocks with good performance are expected to lead the rebound.
   - The market is experiencing a rotation from overvalued sectors to undervalued ones.

4. **Market Sentiment and Predictions:**
   - The market is expected to find a bottom around 337.5 points, with a potential rebound to 345 points.
   - The best scenario is for the market to bottom by Wednesday and rebound by Thursday.
   - The market needs to end the momentum-driven phase, with individual investors' focus on secondary batteries needing to shift.

5. **Credit and Leverage Concerns:**
   - High levels of credit in the market, particularly in secondary batteries, need to be addressed.
   - The unwinding of leveraged positions is necessary for market stabilization.

6. **Foreign Investors' Strategy:**
   - Foreign investors are not significantly increasing their futures selling, indicating potential for program buying later.
   - The market's normalization depends on the reduction of foreign futures selling.

7. **Sector-Specific Insights:**
   - Secondary batteries and related sectors are overvalued and need further correction.
   - The market is expected to rotate towards sectors with solid fundamentals and undervaluation.
   - The automotive sector faces challenges due to electric vehicle sales not meeting expectations.

8. **Market Strategy:**
   - Investors should focus on stocks with strong fundamentals and undervaluation.
   - The market is undergoing a natural normalization process, with opportunities in sectors like finance, insurance, and undervalued tech stocks.

9. **Conclusion:**
   - The market is in a phase of correction and normalization, with a focus on reducing leverage and shifting towards fundamentally strong sectors.
   - Investors are advised to be cautious with overvalued sectors and focus on long-term value opportunities.
