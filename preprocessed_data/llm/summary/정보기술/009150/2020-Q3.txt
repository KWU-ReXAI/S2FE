### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:

1. **Samsung Electro-Mechanics' Business Strategy:**
   - Samsung Electro-Mechanics is restructuring by withdrawing from non-core businesses, including the RFPCB (Rigid-Flex Printed Circuit Board) sector, to focus on core areas like 5G and MLCC (Multi-Layer Ceramic Capacitors).
   - The RFPCB business is significant, with an annual revenue of approximately 500 billion KRW.

2. **Impact on FPCB Market:**
   - The withdrawal from RFPCB is expected to benefit companies involved in FPCB (Flexible Printed Circuit Board) production, as these components are crucial for connecting OLEDs and mainboards, as well as antennas and mainboards.
   - The FPCB market is poised for growth due to the increasing adoption of OLED technology over LCDs.

3. **Market Dynamics and Stock Performance:**
   - The FPCB sector has faced challenges, including the 2018 iPhone X sales slump and the US-China trade tensions, which led to a stagnant stock performance for FPCB companies.
   - Recent shifts, such as increased Samsung phone sales in India and Huawei's market share decline, are creating a more favorable environment for FPCB growth.

4. **Highlighted Companies:**
   - **BH Co., Ltd.:**
     - BH is identified as a key player in the FPCB market, with a target stock price increase from 21,000 KRW to 29,000 KRW.
     - Despite a potential dip in Q3 performance, BH is expected to achieve record earnings in 2021, with significant revenue and profit growth.

   - **Interflex Co., Ltd.:**
     - Interflex is another significant FPCB company, with a target stock price increase from 13,650 KRW to 16,000 KRW.
     - The company is expected to benefit from the integration of digitizers in foldable phones, as the market shifts from the Galaxy Note series to foldable devices.

5. **Future Outlook:**
   - The FPCB market is expected to expand with the growing adoption of OLED technology and the potential for increased demand due to Samsung's business restructuring.
   - Companies like BH and Interflex are positioned to capitalize on these trends, with potential stock price increases as market conditions improve.
