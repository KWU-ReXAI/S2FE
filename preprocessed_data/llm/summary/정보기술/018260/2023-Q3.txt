#### Company Overview
- **Establishment and Listing**: Samsung SDS was established on May 1, 1985, and listed on the Korea Exchange on November 14, 2014.
- **Business Segments**: The company operates primarily in two sectors: IT services and logistics.

#### IT Services
- **Subdivisions**: IT services are divided into Business Solutions and Cloud ITO services.
- **Key Offerings**:
  - **SI (System Integration)**: Focuses on digital transformation using AI, blockchain, and other IT technologies to enhance business performance and productivity.
  - **ITO (IT Outsourcing)**: Provides IT outsourcing services to enhance business competitiveness, leveraging over 35 years of IT operation expertise.
  - **Cloud Services**: Offers end-to-end IT services on the cloud, including consulting, migration, and integrated operations, with a focus on enterprise cloud services and hybrid multi-cloud solutions.

#### Logistics
- **Platform and Services**: Utilizes the self-developed logistics management platform, Cello, and SCM consulting to execute global integrated logistics.
- **Customer Base**: Major clients include Samsung Electronics' global operations, with ongoing efforts to expand to other affiliates and global customers.

#### Financial Performance
- **Revenue Breakdown (2023 H1)**:
  - Logistics accounts for 55% of revenue.
  - IT services account for 44%, with ITO at 23%, cloud services at 12%, and SI at 8%.
- **Geographical Revenue**: Highest revenue from domestic sales, followed by the Americas, Asia, Africa, Europe, and China.

#### Valuation and Growth Analysis
- **IT Services**: Despite lower revenue share compared to logistics, IT services have higher profit margins and less volatility, making them crucial for valuation.

- **Growth Challenges**: IT services have seen revenue growth but declining operating profits since 2020, attributed to rising labor costs in SI and ITO sectors.
- **Cloud Services**: Expected to drive IT service revenue growth, with potential for strong operating leverage as sales increase.

#### Future Outlook
- **IT Services**: Long-term growth in IT services could lead to a significant rebound in profits and stock prices.
- **Logistics**: Without demonstrating long-term growth, the logistics segment's impact on valuation is expected to remain limited.
