### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:

- **Current Stock Performance:**
  - LG Display's stock was purchased at 35,050 KRW, but has been on a decline since late July, currently at 29,150 KRW.
  - The stock's decline has raised concerns among investors about future performance and strategies.

- **Company Strategy and Investments:**
  - LG Display is focusing on expanding its capabilities in both large and small-to-medium OLED markets.
  - The company announced a significant expansion plan worth 7.8 trillion KRW, with investments in both large and small-to-medium OLEDs.
  - Financial support from major companies like Google and Apple is seen as a positive signal for future customer acquisition.

- **Market Competition and Risks:**
  - Samsung Display is a dominant player in the small-to-medium OLED market, having invested over 10 trillion KRW in recent years.
  - LG Display's entry into the small-to-medium OLED market is seen as risky due to potential delays in achieving production efficiency.
  - The investment scale in small-to-medium OLEDs by LG Display is significantly lower than Samsung's, raising concerns about competitive disparity.

- **Investment Recommendations:**
  - The current market situation suggests a conservative approach towards LG Display for the remainder of the year.
  - Investors are advised to reduce their holdings by half if the stock rebounds to around 32,000 KRW.
  - Monitoring the OLED market developments in the first half of the next year is recommended for potential portfolio adjustments.

- **Conclusion:**
  - The analysis suggests cautious optimism, with potential for stock recovery in the short term and strategic adjustments in the long term based on market conditions.
