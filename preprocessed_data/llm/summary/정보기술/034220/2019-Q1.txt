### Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:

- **Transition from LCD to OLED:**
  - South Korean display companies are shifting focus from LCD to OLED technology.
  - Chinese companies, particularly BOE, are rapidly catching up in the OLED market.

- **Market Share and Competition:**
  - In the previous year, South Korean companies dominated the smartphone OLED market with a 99% share.
  - BOE has surpassed LG Display with a 9.4% market share, primarily by supplying flexible OLED panels to Huawei.

- **Investment and Production Plans:**
  - Chinese display companies are heavily investing in OLED production facilities.
  - By 2023, China is expected to produce 38% of the global OLED supply.
  - BOE plans to convert part of its production line to touch-integrated OLED and start mass production next year.

- **Strategic Partnerships and Supply Chain:**
  - Apple plans to use Samsung's touch-integrated OLED for new iPhones but may include BOE in its supply chain if BOE succeeds in mass production.
  - Huawei maintains relationships with both Samsung Display and BOE, with potential for BOE to take over Huawei's supply due to strong government backing.

- **Challenges for South Korean Companies:**
  - BOE has become the world's leading LCD company and is aggressively pursuing the OLED market.
  - South Korean companies maintain competitiveness in large LCD panels, but BOE's new 10.5-generation line could challenge this.
  - LG Display's revenue heavily relies on LCD sales, leading to financial difficulties.

- **Future Strategies:**
  - LG Display aims to increase OLED TV panel sales to 10 million units by 2021, raising OLED revenue share to over 40%.
  - The challenge remains in finding large customers beyond Apple, affecting negotiation power and pricing.

- **Conclusion:**
  - The South Korean display industry faces intense competition from China.
  - The need for strategic partnerships and innovation is crucial for maintaining market position.
