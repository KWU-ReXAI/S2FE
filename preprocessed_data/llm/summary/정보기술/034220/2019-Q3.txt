**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

1. **Context and Background:**
   - LG Display is implementing voluntary retirement for office workers, following a similar initiative for production workers.
   - This is the first time in 12 years that such a measure is being taken for office staff.

2. **Details of the Voluntary Retirement Plan:**
   - The plan targets office workers with over five years of service, excluding those in key technology areas like OLED.
   - Compensation includes a severance package equivalent to 26 months of basic salary.
   - The retirement process is expected to conclude by the end of December.

3. **Previous Measures:**
   - Last month, LG Display conducted voluntary retirement for production workers, with women comprising 60% of those who opted for it.

4. **Reasons for the Voluntary Retirement:**
   - The primary reason is to address deteriorating financial performance.
   - The company is undergoing a high-intensity emergency management strategy to improve its financial situation.
   - LG Display has been affected by an oversupply of LCDs due to low-cost competition from China, leading to significant financial losses.

5. **Financial Performance:**
   - In the third quarter, LG Display reported sales of 5.8217 trillion KRW and an operating loss of 436.7 billion KRW.
   - The cumulative loss for the year amounts to 937.5 billion KRW.

6. **Leadership and Strategic Changes:**
   - Following the appointment of the new president, Jeong Ho-young, in September, the company initiated the voluntary retirement process for production workers.
   - Plans to reduce executive and managerial positions by 25% were also announced.

**Conclusion:**
LG Display is undertaking significant restructuring efforts, including voluntary retirements and management reductions, to combat financial losses exacerbated by market conditions and competition. These measures are part of a broader strategy to stabilize and improve the company's economic performance.
