**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

- **Project Overview:**
  - SKON, a major player in the battery industry, is constructing a new plant in Seosan, South Korea, with a budget of 1.5 trillion won, expected to be completed by 2025.

- **Construction Halt:**
  - The construction was abruptly halted for four days due to a lack of board approval for additional construction costs.
  - SKON unilaterally informed the construction company of the halt, causing disruptions.

- **Impact on Stakeholders:**
  - The sudden halt affected construction partners and workers, who faced uncertainty and financial strain.
  - Hyundai Construction, the main contractor, was not informed about the resumption of work or compensation for the halt.

- **Economic Implications:**
  - Partner companies, which had invested in manpower and equipment for the long-term project, faced potential financial losses.
  - Workers, particularly those relying on daily wages, were concerned about their livelihoods due to the unexpected work stoppage.

- **Resolution:**
  - Following media coverage, SKON held an emergency board meeting and decided to resume construction.
  - However, no specific compensation plan for the affected parties was announced.

**Logical Structure:**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Description of the halted construction project by SKON in Seosan.

2. **Reason for Halt:**
   - Lack of board approval for additional costs leading to the suspension.

3. **Stakeholder Impact:**
   - Effects on construction companies, partner firms, and workers.

4. **Response and Resolution:**
   - SKON's decision to resume construction after media attention.
   - Absence of a detailed compensation plan for affected parties.
