2. **Technological Features and Advantages:**
   - OLED displays use self-emissive organic materials, offering superior image quality and flexibility compared to LCDs.
   - Samsung's new OLED TVs feature enhanced contrast technology, providing brighter highlights and deeper blacks.
   - OLED technology is noted for its thinness, flexibility, and lower power consumption, making it a next-generation display technology.

3. **Market Trends and Strategic Shifts:**
   - The global TV market is experiencing a shift towards premium OLED TVs, especially as the LCD market faces price competition from Chinese manufacturers.
   - Companies are focusing on expanding the OLED TV market to counteract the global economic downturn affecting appliance sales.

4. **Technological and Economic Challenges:**
   - The technological gap between South Korean and Chinese OLED manufacturers is narrowing, particularly in smaller-sized displays.
   - Reducing production costs is a critical challenge, as OLED TVs are currently more expensive than LCDs.
   - South Korean companies need to focus on cost reduction through technological advancements.

   - The South Korean government has designated the display industry as a national strategic industry.
