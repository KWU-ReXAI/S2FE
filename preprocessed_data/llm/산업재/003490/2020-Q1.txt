**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

1. **Korean Air's Capital Increase:**
   - Korean Air is planning a capital increase of up to 1 trillion KRW.
   - This move is part of efforts to secure liquidity support of 1.2 trillion KRW through state-backed financial institutions.

2. **Board Meeting Decisions:**
   - The board meeting discussed the potential capital increase, likely to be executed through a shareholder allocation followed by a public offering.
   - Hanjin KAL, holding about 30% of Korean Air's shares, is expected to hold a board meeting to decide on participating in the capital increase.

3. **Exclusion of Asset Sales:**
   - The sale of in-flight meal and aircraft maintenance businesses, previously considered as part of self-help measures, was not discussed in the board meeting.

4. **Financial Disclosure:**
   - Korean Air plans to announce the specifics of the capital increase, including timing and scale, after the stock market closes.

5. **First Quarter Financial Performance:**
   - Korean Air is expected to report an operating loss exceeding 200 billion KRW for the first quarter.
   - This marks the first quarterly loss in 18 quarters, primarily due to a 90% reduction in international flights caused by COVID-19.

6. **Cargo Revenue:**
   - Despite the losses, cargo revenue, accounting for about 20% of total sales, has performed well, potentially mitigating the overall operating loss.

7. **Future Outlook:**
   - Korean Air plans to resume operations on 32 out of 110 international routes next month, but the second quarter is still expected to be challenging.

**Cited Examples and Data:**
- Capital increase of up to 1 trillion KRW.
- Liquidity support of 1.2 trillion KRW.
- Operating loss of over 200 billion KRW in Q1.
- 90% reduction in international flights.
- Cargo revenue constitutes 20% of total sales.
- Resumption of 32 international routes out of 110 planned.
