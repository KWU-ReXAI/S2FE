**Summary of Key Economic Ideas and Arguments:**

- **Reopening of Air Travel Routes:**
  - The Incheon-Hawaii air route, which was closed due to the COVID-19 pandemic, is set to reopen after 1 year and 7 months.
  - Korean Air plans to resume regular flights on this route starting in November, with a schedule of three flights per week.

- **Regulatory and Operational Plans:**
  - Korean Air has applied for route operation approval from the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, and Transport in October.
  - There are plans to increase the frequency of flights to five times a week by December.

- **Hawaii's Entry Regulations:**
  - The Hawaiian government currently imposes a mandatory 10-day quarantine for incoming travelers.
  - However, travelers can be exempted from quarantine if they present a negative COVID-19 test result from designated institutions, such as Yonsei University Severance Hospital.

1. **Introduction of Route Reopening:**
   - Announcement of the reopening of the Incheon-Hawaii air route.

2. **Operational Details:**
   - Korean Air's schedule and plans for increasing flight frequency.

3. **Regulatory Process:**
   - Application for route operation approval and future expansion plans.

4. **Entry Requirements and Exemptions:**
   - Current quarantine requirements in Hawaii and conditions for exemption.
