1. **Historical Context and Technological Evolution:**
   - The introduction of electricity in the 1880s saw a debate between Thomas Edison’s direct current (DC) and Nikola Tesla’s alternating current (AC).
   - AC was initially favored due to its ease of voltage regulation and economic advantages for long-distance transmission.

2. **Re-emergence of Direct Current (DC):**
   - Recent technological advancements have minimized power loss, making long-distance DC transmission feasible.
   - This has renewed interest in DC, particularly through High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) technology.

3. **HVDC Technology and Industry Developments:**
   - HVDC is a next-generation power transmission technology that enables efficient long-distance and high-capacity power transmission, including through submarine cables.
   - The global HVDC market is dominated by companies like Siemens, ABB, and GE.

4. **Domestic Developments in South Korea:**
   - L.S. Industrial Systems (LSIS) has invested significantly in HVDC technology, establishing a dedicated plant in Busan.
   - LSIS has completed the development of key HVDC components and has conducted successful pilot operations.

5. **Strategic Projects and Market Expansion:**
   - LSIS has undertaken significant projects, such as the Bukdangjin-Godeok project and the Donghae-Singapyeong HVDC conversion facility.
   - The company aims to expand its presence in the global market by leveraging HVDC technology.

6. **Potential for Regional Integration:**
   - There is optimism about connecting the power grids of North and South Korea, and potentially creating an East Asian supergrid involving Russia, China, and Japan.
   - A supergrid would facilitate international power trade and the integration of renewable energy sources.

7. **Complementary Technologies:**
   - LSIS developed Static Var Compensator (SVC) technology in 2015, which enhances power transmission stability by compensating for power losses in AC systems.
   - This technology complements HVDC by addressing the limitations of AC power loss.

8. **Future Plans:**
   - LSIS plans to expand its product range across the HVDC spectrum within the next three years.
   - The company aims to become a global leader in both DC and AC power systems.
